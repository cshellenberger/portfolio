Highlighted Projects

Flexible Robotic Surgical Instruments

Company: Titan Medical

Role: Principal Engineer, Instrumentation

Challenge: Develop a suite of articulating robotic surgical instruments from concept to production for a novel surgical robot platform.

Approach: Managed a 6-person team, collaborating closely with surgeons and marketing to define product and engineering requirements. Established and managed an internal pilot manufacturing line and machine shop. Led the transition to scalable, cost-efficient production methods.

Results: Successfully brought multiple instrument designs through the development lifecycle, addressing complex mechanical and user interface challenges. Established robust manufacturing processes.

Automated Pneumatic Press

Company: Titan Medical

Manufacturing Automation

What: Design, fabricate, and program a fixture to automate the crimping process for surgical instruments.

How: Mechanical Design in SolidWorks, OTS Press (Janesville), Clearpath servo positioner, Keyence Light Curtain Sensors, Allen Bradley PLC + HMI, Ladder Logic control.

Results: Press implemented in production, used on over 300 instruments. Achieved crimp position repeatability of +/-.002".

Automated Heat Shrink Station

Company: Titan Medical

Manufacturing Automation

What: Reduce the amount of time it takes to fill tubes with oil and minimize human operator error.

How: Mechanical Design in SolidWorks, 2-Axis Servo Control, Automatic Shutoff, Hardware: AB Micro850 + HMI, Software: Ladder Logic.

Results: Achieved no plastic part deformation, eliminated a high-risk failure mode, and improved manufacturing throughput with "walk away" capability.

Vision Inspection of Ligation Clips

Company: Teleflex Inc.

High-Volume Manufacturing Automation

What: Create an inspection system for implantable ligation clips, compatible with high-volume production (1M/month).

How: Component Design in SolidWorks, PLC HMI (Allen Bradley Micro850), Ladder Logic, Keyence Vision System. System included a bowl feeder, linear rail, pneumatic pick and place, vision inspection from 2 angles, and an automatic sorting system.

Results: Developed a working prototype that caught 99% of defects, crucial for quality assurance in high-volume medical device manufacturing.

Laser Weld Rotary Stage

Simple Manufacturing Fixture

What: Create a fixture to rotate a sensitive assembly during laser welding, involving manual insertion and automated rotation.

How: Component Design in SolidWorks, Control M-Drive Stepper, Laser Welder (LaserStar).

Results: Implemented in pilot production and used on 100% of devices built, ensuring consistent weld quality.

Manual Presses with Custom Tooling

Simple Manufacturing Fixtures

What: Create a set of simple manual presses for multiple assembly operations on the production line.

How: Design in SolidWorks, Schmidt Presses, Custom tooling made by a local machine shop.

Results: Implemented in pilot production and used on 100% of devices built, providing essential support for assembly tasks.
